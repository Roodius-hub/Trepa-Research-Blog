Binary vs Precision

Two fundamentally different payout models

Binary Markets

Question Format
"Will BTC be above $100K on Dec 31?"
YES or NO
Payout Structure
All or nothing. Right side = 100% payout.
Wrong side = 0% payout.
You buy YES at $0.60. If BTC hits $100,001, you win $1.00 per share (+67% return). If it hits $99,999, you lose everything.
Key Metrics
Win Condition Directional
Skill Measured Conviction
Variance High
Liquidity Model Orderbook/AMM
Strengths
Simple to understand (yes/no)
Lock in your odds before resolution
Deep liquidity on popular events
Decades of research backing the model
Weaknesses
High variance (win big or lose all)
Only captures threshold crossing
No partial credit for near-misses
51% confidence = 99% confidence in payout

Precision Markets

Question Format
"What will BTC price be at exactly 3:00 PM?"
Numerical estimate
Payout Structure
Proportional to accuracy. Closer = bigger share.
Win if below median error.
You predict $97,234. Actual: $97,180. Your error: $54. If median error is $120, you win and get paid proportionally to how much closer you were vs others.
Key Metrics
Win Condition Accuracy
Skill Measured Calibration
Variance Lower
Liquidity Model Parimutuel Pool
Strengths
Partial credit for near-misses
Captures full distribution, not just probability
Lower variance for skilled forecasters
No liquidity fragmentation across strikes
Enables micro-interval resolution (60s)
Weaknesses
Higher cognitive load (need actual numbers)
Can't lock in odds (parimutuel opacity)
Limited to continuous outcome events
Less validation at ultra-fast intervals
